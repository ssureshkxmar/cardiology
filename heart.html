üî¨ Anatomy and Structure

The human heart is a remarkable muscular organ approximately the size of a closed fist, weighing between 250-350 grams in adults. Located in the mediastinum of the thoracic cavity, slightly left of the body's midline, it serves as the central pump of the cardiovascular system. The heart consists of four chambers: two upper atria (right and left) and two lower ventricles (right and left), separated by a muscular wall called the septum.

üî¥ Right Atrium

  • Receives deoxygenated blood from body
  • Contains sinoatrial (SA) node
  • Connects to right ventricle via tricuspid valve

üîµ Right Ventricle

  • Pumps blood to lungs via pulmonary artery
  • Thinner muscular walls than left ventricle
  • Pulmonary valve prevents backflow

üî¥ Left Atrium

  • Receives oxygenated blood from lungs
  • Connects to left ventricle via mitral valve
  • Maintains pressure for efficient filling

üîµ Left Ventricle

  • Strongest chamber - pumps to entire body
  • Thickest muscular walls (8-12mm)
  • Aortic valve controls blood flow to aorta
üíì The Cardiac Cycle: Each heartbeat consists of two phases - systole (contraction) and diastole (relaxation). During systole, ventricles contract to pump blood out of the heart. During diastole, chambers relax and fill with blood. This cycle repeats approximately 100,000 times per day, pumping about 7,570 liters of blood daily.

ü©∫ Cardiovascular Health

Maintaining heart health is crucial for overall well-being. The heart's remarkable endurance and efficiency depend on proper nutrition, regular exercise, stress management, and avoiding harmful habits like smoking. Regular cardiovascular check-ups and monitoring of blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and heart rate are essential preventive measures.

üí™ Exercise Benefits

  • Strengthens cardiac muscle
  • Improves circulation and oxygen delivery
  • Lowers blood pressure and cholesterol
  • Reduces risk of heart disease by 35%

ü•ó Nutritional Support

  • Omega-3 fatty acids for heart health
  • Antioxidants reduce inflammation
  • Fiber helps manage cholesterol
  • Potassium regulates blood pressure

  • High cholesterol levels
